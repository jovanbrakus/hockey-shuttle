1. **Authentic Teen Voices** - Minimal cringe, characters sound like real 17-18 year olds
2. **Smart Protagonists** - Sophia and Ethan are brilliant and it shows in problem-solving, not just grades
3. **Sports Integration** - Badminton and hockey aren't set dressing, they're integral to character identity
4. **Slow Burn Romance** - 8 months from reconnection to dating feels earned and real
5. **Actual Stakes** - Controlling parents, emancipation, life-altering choices are REAL obstacles
6. **Consequences Matter** - Choices have impacts, growth takes time, problems don't disappear
7. **Theme Consistency** - "Choosing yourself" woven through every major decision
8. **No Breakup Drama** - The relationship faces external obstacles, not manufactured couple conflict
9. **Secondary Characters** - Maya, Jordan, Tyler, Caroline all have depth and growth
10. **Parental Complexity** - Parents feel like real people with their own arcs, not just obstacles

## SERIES WEAKNESSES

### What Holds It Back From Perfection:

1. **Crisis Fatigue** - Relentless escalation becomes exhausting around Episodes 5-7
2. **Repetitive Obstacles** - Same conflicts resurface (NHL offer presented 3 times)
3. **Robert Price Villainy** - Controlling parent becomes cartoonishly evil by Episode 8
4. **Pacing Imbalance** - Too much suffering, not enough breathing room to enjoy victories
5. **Finale Structure** - Episode 10 reads more like Season 2 Episode 1 than satisfying finale
6. **Resolution vs Setup** - Season ends on cliffhangers without enough closure
7. **Missing Mystery** - Episode 3's stalker texts never resolved (who was the unknown number?)

**1. Episode 3, Chapter 4 - Fix Time Skip Structure**

- Don't present NHL choice THREE times - once in June is enough
- Trust "can they survive success" hook without piling on every possible conflict
- Finale needs RESOLUTION not just new setup

- He's cartoonishly villainous by Episode 8 (14 months surveillance)
- Either: show moments where we understand his fear/motivation

- Episodes 3-5 are relentlessly stressful

- Example: Episode 7's spring break was perfect - more of that

**5. Unknown Number Mystery - RESOLVE IT**
- Episode 3 introduces stalker texts
- They just... disappear
- Either cut them entirely OR resolve who it was
- Can't introduce mystery subplot and ghost it
