# WORLD-BUILDING QUICK REFERENCE GUIDE
## Episode 4 "Matchup" - Winnipeg Winter Setting Bible

### WINNIPEG WEATHER CALENDAR (Nov-Jan)

**Late November (Episode Start)**
- Temperature: -10°C to -15°C (feels like -20°C with wind)
- Sunrise: 7:45 AM / Sunset: 4:35 PM
- Snow: Usually 15-30cm accumulated
- Special: First "real" cold snaps, winter tire rush

**Early December (Formal)**
- Temperature: -15°C to -20°C (wind chill to -30°C)
- Sunrise: 8:00 AM / Sunset: 4:25 PM
- Snow: 30-45cm accumulated
- Special: Christmas decorations up, shortened daylight noticeable

**Late December (Christmas/New Year's)**
- Temperature: -20°C to -30°C common
- Sunrise: 8:15 AM / Sunset: 4:30 PM
- Snow: 45-60cm accumulated
- Special: Extreme cold warnings, everything crystalline

**Early January (Episode End)**
- Temperature: -25°C to -35°C possible
- Sunrise: 8:10 AM / Sunset: 4:45 PM
- Snow: 60cm+ accumulated
- Special: "Winterpeg" in full force, survival mode

## SENSORY CHEAT SHEET

### IF IT'S -10°C to -15°C:
- Breath visible but dissipates quickly
- Need gloves but can survive brief exposure
- Snow squeaks slightly when walking
- Cars start reluctantly but start

### IF IT'S -15°C to -25°C:
- Breath forms thick clouds
- Exposed skin hurts within minutes
- Snow squeaks loudly (styrofoam sound)
- Cars need block heaters or won't start
- Indoor glasses fog immediately

### IF IT'S -25°C to -35°C:
- Breath freezes on scarves/collars
- Exposed skin can freeze in under 10 minutes
- Snow sounds like breaking glass
- Even plugged-in cars struggle
- Metal burns to touch
- Indoor/outdoor transition is shocking

## WINNIPEG CULTURAL MARKERS

### ALWAYS PRESENT:
- Jets hockey references (flags, jerseys, conversations)
- Weather as #1 small talk topic
- Everyone knows everyone through 2-3 degrees
- French on signs/announcements
- Indigenous presence (largest urban pop in Canada)

### WINTER SPECIFIC:
- Remote car starters everywhere
- Emergency kits in every trunk
- "Winnipeg handshake" (helping stuck cars)
- Vitamin D deficiency jokes
- "At least it's a dry cold" (lie)
- Comparing this winter to worse winters

### NEIGHBORHOOD CODES:
- **River Heights:** Established, professional, Jewish community
- **Wolseley:** Artistic, progressive, organic everything
- **St. Vital:** French quarter, middle class, family-oriented
- **Exchange District:** Hipster central, artists, students
- **Transcona:** Working class, tight-knit, "separate city"

## TEEN LIFE IN WINNIPEG WINTER

### GETTING AROUND:
- Everyone drives by 16 (necessity not luxury)
- Warming up cars = hangout time
- Designated driver crucial (too cold to walk)
- Parents track via Find My Phone
- "Text when you get there" is law

### SOCIAL SPACES:
- House parties > outdoor anything
- Basement hangouts standard
- Mall food courts after school
- Coffee shops near universities
- Arena lobbies during games

### WINTER DATING:
- Car dates (warm, private)
- Coffee shop meetups
- Mall wandering
- Hockey game "dates"
- Netflix legitimately (too cold for chill)

## DIALOGUE QUIRKS

### WINNIPEG SPECIFIC:
- "Hey?" at end of sentences
- "Supposed to" = "s'posed ta"
- Weather warnings as greetings
- Directions by landmarks not streets
- Time measured in "before/after the Jets game"

### WINTER COMPLAINTS HIERARCHY:
1. "It's not even that cold" (-10°C)
2. "Now it's cold" (-20°C)
3. "This is stupid" (-30°C)
4. Silent suffering (-35°C+)

### TEEN SPEECH PATTERNS:
- Mix of American TV and Canadian specific
- "Literally" but also "bloody"
- Hockey slang bleeds into everything
- Indigenous words in common use (bannock, etc.)

## EMERGENCY WORLD-BUILDING FIXES

**If scene feels generic, add:**
1. Specific Winnipeg location/landmark
2. Weather affecting the action
3. Local business/reference
4. Sensory detail (usually smell or sound)
5. Someone complaining about winter

**If dialogue feels flat, add:**
1. Weather comment
2. Jets reference
3. Local geography mention
4. "Hey?" as question ender
5. Comparison to "last winter"

**If setting invisible, add:**
1. Temperature effect on characters
2. Lighting (darkness at 4:30 PM!)
3. Sound that's winter-specific
4. Clothing/gear struggle
5. Indoor/outdoor transition moment

## MOST COMMON WORLD-BUILDING MISTAKES TO AVOID

❌ Characters outside too long without consequence
❌ Forgetting darkness (sunset 4:30 PM in December!)
❌ Clean cars in winter
❌ Walking distances in extreme cold
❌ No mention of layered clothing
❌ Generic "cold" without specifics
❌ Missing the constant weather talk
❌ Ignoring how cold affects everything
❌ No local references/landmarks
❌ Forgetting the Jets exist
