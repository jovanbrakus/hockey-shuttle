# Teen Culture Consultant Review: Episode 7 "Spring Training"

## Executive Summary

Episode 7 presents an emotionally compelling story with strong legal and interpersonal drama. The relationship progression feels genuine, and the characters' emotional responses are authentic. However, the episode suffers from **significant contemporary teen culture authenticity issues**, primarily around:

1. **Overly formal communication style** - Teens in 2025 don't communicate this formally, even smart ones
2. **Minimal contemporary language** - Almost no current teen slang or speech patterns
3. **Dated digital communication** - Email usage feels old-fashioned; texting style is too proper
4. **Absent social media presence** - Despite the plot revolving around teen lives, social media is nearly invisible
5. **Missing friend group dynamics** - No group chats, minimal digital friend presence
6. **Lack of cultural touchstones** - No music, memes, or pop culture references that ground it in 2025

The characters read more like articulate young adults from 2010-2015 rather than contemporary 2025 teenagers. While the emotional authenticity is strong, the cultural authenticity needs significant work to feel current and relatable to actual Gen Z readers.

The episode lacks contemporary slang and speech markers that would ground it in 2025. Teenagers use filler words, hedging language, and contemporary slang even in serious conversations.

- "ngl" (not gonna lie)
- "lowkey" / "highkey"

### Recommendation:
