# EPISODE 1: "Then and Now"

## Basic Information
- **Episode Number**: S01E01
- **Page Count**: 80 pages (20,000 words)
- **Timeline**: September, Junior year begins
- **Primary Locations**: Flashback to small-town rink (age 9), Chen family home, Sophia's new school orientation, St. Paul's High School, Bell MTS Iceplex
- **Weather**: Early September, golden autumn light, crisp but warm

## Synopsis
Sophia Chen's life splits in two on the same day: flashbacks reveal the childhood friendship she lost when Ethan moved away at age 9, while present-day shows her arrival in Winnipeg as a terrified new senior who only knows one person—the boy who used to share his skate guards with her. When Ethan Price walks into her homeroom, he's not the 9-year-old with scraped knees anymore—he's the school's hockey star with a perfect girlfriend and a perfect life. But the way he says her name, like he's been waiting to say it again, makes her wonder if some things never really change.

## Story Components

### Main Plot (60%): New Girl Survival
Sophia navigates first week at new school in senior year—the most terrifying social scenario imaginable. Registration, orientation, trying to figure out where anything is, who anyone is. Ethan finds her in the chaos, becomes her guide. He introduces her to his world: girlfriend Becca (popular, genuinely nice, making this complicated), hockey teammates, the school's social landscape. By episode's end, Sophia has tenuous foothold in Winnipeg life, entirely because of Ethan.

### Character Arc (25%): Outsider Seeking Belonging
Sophia arrives performing "confident new student" but is internally terrified. Flashbacks show the confident 9-year-old she used to be around Ethan. Present-day shows how much she's learned to armor herself. Arc: Performs confidence → genuine terror revealed → first moment of real belonging (with Ethan, it's like no time passed).

### Series Arc (15%): Foundation of Central Relationship
Establishes the childhood bond that makes their present connection deeper than "just met." They have history. They have inside jokes from age 9 that still land. They have a foundation that makes the chemistry feel inevitable, not sudden.

## Romance Beats

### The Recognition (Chapter 1)
Ethan walks into homeroom, sees her, and for a moment the entire cafeteria noise disappears. He says "Sophia Chen?" like he's confirming she's real. She says "You got tall." It's awkward and perfect.

### The Remembered Details (Chapter 2)
He remembers she likes chocolate milk, not regular. Brings her one at lunch without asking. She remembers he used to tap his pencil three times before writing—he still does. These tiny recognitions feel enormous.

### The Easy Conversation (Chapter 3)
In his truck after school (he offers to drive her home), they talk for 45 minutes in her driveway. Neither notices the time. It feels like falling into old rhythm, except the rhythm is now charged with something neither will name.

### The Girlfriend Introduction (Chapter 3)
Becca appears. She's beautiful, kind, perfect. Sophia feels the distance between Ethan's world and hers. But also: Ethan's hand was on the back of Sophia's chair before Becca arrived. He moved it.

### The Cliffhanger Setup (Chapter 4)
Friday night, Ethan texts: "Want to come to my game tomorrow? I know you hate hockey but..." And Sophia, who does not hate hockey, who used to love skating with him, realizes she's been hiding part of herself. She texts back: "I'll be there."

## Key Scenes

1. **Cold Open: Age 9 on Ice** (2 pages)
   - Flashback: Nine-year-old Sophia and Ethan on community rink
   - He teaches her a crossover, she's all determination
   - His mom calls him—time to go
   - "Tomorrow?" she asks. "Tomorrow," he promises.
   - But tomorrow never comes—his family moves that week

2. **Present: First Day Chaos** (8 pages)
   - Sophia lost in new school, holding printed schedule like a lifeline
   - Trying to project confidence, internally panicking
   - Homeroom: Ethan walks in, double-take, "Sophia Chen?"
   - Time stops—he's not nine anymore, but somehow still exactly him

3. **The Cafeteria Navigation** (10 pages)
   - Ethan guides her through lunch politics
   - Introduces her to his table: Becca (girlfriend), Jordan (teammate), Maya (who's also in Sophia's badminton intake)
   - Sophia performs "friendly new girl," Ethan keeps looking at her like he's solving a puzzle
   - Maya: "How do you two know each other?" The answer is longer than they can explain

4. **Truck Conversation #1** (12 pages)
   - Ethan offers ride home after first day
   - They should talk about school, schedules, normal things
   - Instead: "Remember when you fell through the ice on that pond and I thought you died?" "I was in six inches of water." "Still thought you died."
   - Forty-five minutes pass without them noticing
   - Sophia's mom knocks on truck window—"You planning to come inside?"

5. **Hockey Introduction** (8 pages)
   - Ethan at practice, Sophia watches from stands with Maya (bonding)
   - Sophia sees the player Ethan is: focused, aggressive, excellent
   - He's performing here too, she realizes
   - After practice, he skates to boards: "You stayed"
   - "You're good." "You sound surprised." "I'm not. You were always going to be good."

6. **Friday Night Text** (5 pages)
   - Sophia in her room, week one complete, exhausted
   - Processing: new school, new city, Ethan with girlfriend, weird chemistry she's definitely imagining
   - Text from Ethan: "Want to come to my game tomorrow?"
   - She types and deletes four responses
   - Finally: "I'll be there"
   - His response: "Good."

## Chapter Breakdown

### Chapter 1: "Arrival" (20 pages)
- **Cold open**: Flashback to age 9, the last day before he moved
- **Present**: Sophia's first day at St. Paul's High School
- **Scene**: Lost in hallway, homeroom, Ethan's recognition
- **End**: "You're really here" / "I'm really here"

### Chapter 2: "Navigation" (20 pages)
- **Scenes**: Cafeteria introduction, meeting Becca, social landscape mapping
- **Development**: Sophia sees Ethan's life (girlfriend, popularity, hockey centrality)
- **Complication**: She's an outsider in his insider world
- **End**: "Let me drive you home"

### Chapter 3: "Recognition" (20 pages)
- **Scene**: The truck conversation that lasts 45 minutes
- **Development**: They remember each other in tiny, significant details
- **Complication**: This feels like more than friendship, but he has Becca
- **End**: Sophia watches him drive away, realizes she's in trouble

### Chapter 4: "Invitation" (20 pages)
- **Scene**: First week complete, Sophia processing
- **Scene**: Ethan at hockey practice, Sophia watches, sees his excellence
- **Development**: The text invitation to his game
- **Cliffhanger**: She agrees to come—first choice to enter his world fully
- **Final Line**: In stands, game about to start, Ethan looks up, finds her in crowd, and smiles

## Cliffhanger Details
**Type**: The Invitation (Choice)

Sophia could maintain safe distance—be friendly but separate. Coming to his game means entering his world, meeting his friends' girlfriends, being part of his life. The cliffhanger is her decision: she's all in. She's sitting in the stands, game about to start, and Ethan looks directly at her through the glass. The look says everything they haven't said.

**Reader Question**: What does it mean that she came? What does it mean that he looks at her like that when Becca's sitting three rows up?

**Why It Works**: First episode cliffhangers should promise the central relationship dynamic. This one does—they're choosing each other already, even though neither will admit what that means.

## Emotional Goal
**Make readers feel**: The ache of reunion. The "what if" of rediscovering someone who knew you when you were young and unguarded. The danger of easy chemistry when circumstances are complicated.

## What This Episode Makes Readers Feel
1. **Nostalgia**: The flashbacks establish childhood sweetness
2. **Tension**: Present-day chemistry is undeniable and complicated
3. **Investment**: We're rooting for Sophia to find belonging
4. **Anticipation**: Something is building between them
5. **Curiosity**: Desperate to know what happens at that game

# EPISODE 2: "Game Recognize Game"

## Basic Information
- **Episode Number**: S01E02
- **Page Count**: 80 pages (20,000 words)
- **Timeline**: Mid-September to early October, three weeks into school
- **Primary Locations**: Bell MTS Iceplex (hockey games), Badminton Manitoba training center, The Forks (group hangout), Ethan's truck, McNally Robinson bookstore
- **Weather**: September turning cooler, first hints of coming cold

## Synopsis
Sophia becomes part of the Friday night ritual: hockey games, post-game celebrations, the social ecosystem of St. Paul's. She bonds with Maya through badminton, finds her own excellence apart from Ethan's world. But every Friday, she's in those stands. Ethan starts looking for her before every game—his good luck charm, he jokes, except it doesn't feel like a joke. When they end up alone at McNally Robinson (everyone else bailed on study group), three hours disappear into conversation about MIT, science, everything their parents expect them to be vs. who they want to be. Becca notices Ethan talks about Sophia constantly. So does everyone else.

## Story Components

### Main Plot (60%): Integration
Sophia establishes her own identity at St. Paul's: badminton team, advanced placement classes, friend circle forming around Maya. But Friday nights are hockey nights, and she's becoming fixture in the stands. Three games, three victories, and Ethan's superstition grows: Sophia's his luck. Group dynamics form: post-game celebrations, group study sessions, casual hangouts where she's increasingly included.

### Character Arc (25%): Earned Belonging
Sophia stops performing "new girl" and starts inhabiting her space. She's genuinely good at badminton—not best, but among the best. She's brilliant in AP Physics. She has Maya as real friend. But the belonging she feels with Ethan is different—effortless. This episode explores difference between earned belonging (work) and natural belonging (Ethan).

### Series Arc (15%): Deepening Connection
Establishes pattern that will sustain series: they're always finding ways to be alone together. Study group of eight somehow becomes just them. Group hangout at The Forks somehow ends with them walking separate from others. They're not trying to be alone—it just keeps happening. Friends start noticing.

## Romance Beats

### The Pre-Game Ritual (Chapter 1)
Ethan starts texting her before games: "You coming?" She always is. Before third game, he adds: "I play better when you're there." She doesn't know what to do with that information.

### The Victory Hug (Chapter 2)
After a particularly intense game (St. Paul's wins in overtime), Ethan finds Sophia in the crowd and hugs her. It lasts two seconds too long. Becca sees it. Jordan sees it. Everyone sees it.

### The Bookstore Conversation (Chapter 3)
Three hours at McNally Robinson, supposedly studying. Actually: talking about academic dreams, parental pressure, who they want to be. Ethan admits he wants MIT more than NHL. Sophia admits she's terrified of not being the best at badminton. Vulnerabilities exchanged like gifts.

### The Jealous Girlfriend Beat (Chapter 4)
Becca (kind, perceptive Becca) asks Sophia at locker: "How long have you known Ethan?" Not accusatory—genuinely curious. Sophia explains childhood connection. Becca: "That makes sense. He talks about you like you're this person he's been waiting to find again." Sophia doesn't know what to say. Neither does reader.

### The Cliffhanger Setup (Chapter 4)
Group plans Halloween party. Couples costume is tradition. Ethan to Becca: "What should we do?" Becca: "I'm thinking Harley and Joker." But Ethan's looking at Sophia when he asks: "What are you going as?" And Sophia, without thinking: "Maybe a hockey player." The joke lands weird. Everyone laughs but it feels too real.

## Key Scenes

1. **Second Game: The Stands** (7 pages)
   - Sophia sits with Maya, Jordan's girlfriend, other hockey girlfriends
   - Learning the culture: when to cheer, what plays mean, team dynamics
   - Ethan glances up at her between periods
   - After game win, team celebration—Sophia caught up in it
   - Ethan: "You're my good luck charm" (joking tone, serious eyes)

2. **Badminton Practice: Sophia's World** (9 pages)
   - Sophia at her sport, her excellence
   - Maya as doubles partner, natural friendship forming
   - Coach notes Sophia's technical precision but suggests more aggression
   - Sophia realizes she plays badminton like she lives life: controlled, defensive
   - Text from Ethan during practice: "How's shuttle diplomacy?" (Their ongoing pun)

3. **The Forks Group Hangout** (11 pages)
   - Eight people planned, all eight show up
   - Gradual pairing: couples drift together, Maya and Jordan debate art
   - Sophia and Ethan somehow walking separate from group
   - Conversation flows: childhood memories, present observations, future anxieties
   - Becca calls Ethan: "Where are you guys?" They've walked half a mile without noticing

4. **McNally Robinson Study Session** (14 pages)
   - Study group of eight planned; six cancel
   - Just Sophia and Ethan in bookstore café for three hours
   - Books open, studying happens for maybe twenty minutes
   - Rest: Ethan talks about parental pressure (NHL expectation vs. MIT dream)
   - Sophia talks about being "good enough" vs. being "the best"
   - Exchange vulnerabilities neither shares with others
   - Store closing announcement jolts them—"It's been three hours?"

5. **Becca's Question** (8 pages)
   - Sophia at locker, Becca approaches
   - Not confrontational—genuinely curious about Ethan/Sophia history
   - Sophia explains childhood friendship, the move, reconnection
   - Becca: "He's different around you. Lighter."
   - Sophia doesn't know what to do with this information
   - Becca: "I'm glad he has you back." (Is she? Readers question.)

6. **Third Game: The Pattern** (6 pages)
   - Third Friday, third game, Sophia automatically in stands
   - She knows the warm-up routine now, the pre-game music, when to expect him to look up
   - He does look up, finds her immediately, slight smile
   - Game is intense, St. Paul's wins in overtime, Ethan scores winning goal
   - Celebration on ice, then he skates to boards where she's standing
   - Through glass: "You stayed." Through glass: "Where else would I be?"

## Chapter Breakdown

### Chapter 1: "Luck" (20 pages)
- **Scene**: Second hockey game, Sophia integrating into stands culture
- **Development**: Ethan's "good luck charm" comment
- **Complication**: It's not really a joke
- **End**: Post-game hug that lasts too long

### Chapter 2: "Parallel" (20 pages)
- **Scene**: Sophia's badminton practice, establishing her excellence
- **Scene**: The Forks group hangout that becomes accidental pair-off
- **Development**: Both have separate worlds, but keep gravitating together
- **End**: Walking away from group without noticing

### Chapter 3: "Vulnerable" (20 pages)
- **Scene**: McNally Robinson, three hours of conversation
- **Development**: Deep vulnerabilities exchanged
- **Complication**: This intimacy is more than friendship
- **End**: "It's been three hours?" Neither noticed time passing

### Chapter 4: "Observable" (20 pages)
- **Scene**: Becca's question about Ethan/Sophia history
- **Scene**: Third hockey game, pattern established
- **Development**: Others are noticing the connection
- **Cliffhanger**: Halloween party planning—costume tension
- **Final Line**: Ethan to group: "Sophia should come to team Halloween party." Becca: "Of course she should." (The subtext screams.)

## Cliffhanger Details
**Type**: The Observation (Others See It)

The cliffhanger isn't about Sophia and Ethan—it's about everyone else starting to see what they won't admit. Becca's "Of course she should" is loaded with meaning. The Halloween party invitation feels like a test. Readers know parties in YA fiction are where things explode. The question: Will something happen? Will Becca confront? Will they finally admit it?

**Reader Question**: What will happen at Halloween party? Does Becca actually know? How long can they pretend this is just friendship?

**Why It Works**: Raises external pressure. The chemistry isn't just between them anymore—it's observable, commentable, potentially explosive.

## Emotional Goal
**Make readers feel**: The delicious tension of a connection everyone sees except the two people in it. The ache of accidental intimacy—every conversation that goes too long, every look that lingers.

## What This Episode Makes Readers Feel
1. **Anticipation**: Something is building toward Halloween
2. **Frustration**: "Just kiss already" starting to emerge
3. **Anxiety**: Becca is too perceptive, too kind to be villain
4. **Investment**: Sophia's badminton/academic life matters too
5. **Longing**: Those three hours at the bookstore hurt in the best way

# EPISODE 3: "Halloween Revelations"

## Basic Information
- **Episode Number**: S01E03
- **Page Count**: 80 pages (20,000 words)
- **Timeline**: Late October, Halloween week
- **Primary Locations**: Team Halloween party at Jordan's house, costume shop, Ethan's truck (major scene), Sophia's room (processing), late-night diner
- **Weather**: Late October cold, first frost, winter coming

## Synopsis
Halloween party at Jordan's house should be simple: costumes, music, celebration of undefeated season. But Sophia shows up in a vintage Team Canada hockey jersey (ironic costume, she claims), and Ethan can't look away. Becca's Harley Quinn is perfect, his Joker matches, but when party games force truth-or-dare vulnerability, everything shifts. Sophia ends up on the roof with Ethan after he needs air, and in the cold October night, they almost cross the line. Almost. But "almost" is enough to scare them both. He drives her home in loaded silence, and they make a pact: maybe they should have some distance. The episode ends with both of them lying awake, knowing that distance is the last thing they actually want.

## Story Components

### Main Plot (60%): Halloween Party Chaos
Team Halloween party brings together all social circles: hockey team, girlfriends, badminton friends, school crew. Costumes, music, party games escalate into truth-or-dare that gets uncomfortably real. Questions probe relationships, secrets surface, and Ethan needs air. Sophia follows him to roof. Twenty minutes alone in October cold, conversation turns dangerous. Nearly kiss. Pull back. Tension explodes.

### Character Arc (25%): Awareness Becomes Undeniable
Sophia has been telling herself this is friendship, nostalgia, ease of knowing someone from childhood. Halloween party—specifically the roof moment—destroys that rationalization. She wants him. He wants her. They both know it now. The awareness can't be unknowed.

### Series Arc (15%): First Retreat (False Distance)
After roof moment, both panic. They agree to "give each other space"—see each other at school, be friendly, but stop the constant texting, the long conversations, the gravitational pull. This is first attempt at distance. It lasts approximately five days (covered in Episode 4). But attempting distance establishes pattern: they try to stay away, they fail, they try again.

## Romance Beats

### The Costume Moment (Chapter 1)
Sophia arrives in vintage Team Canada jersey (found at thrift store). It's too big, sleeves past her hands, number 9 on back. Ethan sees her, stops mid-sentence in conversation with Jordan. Becca notices he noticed.

### The Party Game (Chapter 2)
Truth-or-dare spins bottle, lands on Ethan. "Truth: who's the most important person in this room to you?" He should say Becca. Everyone expects Becca. He hesitates too long. Bottle moves on, but damage is done.

### The Roof Conversation (Chapter 3)
Ethan needs air, goes to roof. Sophia follows (Maya encourages her to check on him). October night, cold enough to see breath, close enough to share warmth. Conversation about performance, expectations, who they really are. "You're the only person who knew me before I had to be this." Her: "You don't have to be anything with me." The almost-kiss: he leans in, she closes eyes, but he pulls back. "I can't do this to Becca. I can't do this to you."

### The Silent Drive (Chapter 3)
He drives her home. Fifteen-minute drive, maybe fifty words spoken. Every word feels dangerous. In her driveway: "Maybe we need some space." Her: "Yeah. Maybe." Neither moves to leave truck for full minute.

### The Cliffhanger Setup (Chapter 4)
Three days of space. No texting. They pass in hallways, wave politely, nothing more. Sophia is dying. Then: Friday night, his truck pulls up at her house. Text: "Game tonight. You're not there. We're losing. Please come." She's in pajamas. She doesn't care. "Give me five minutes."

## Key Scenes

1. **Pre-Party: Costume Selection** (6 pages)
   - Flashback through week: Sophia and Maya costume shopping
   - Maya pushing for "sexy something," Sophia resistant
   - Finding the Team Canada jersey: "It's ironic. It's a joke."
   - Maya: "That's not why you're buying it."
   - Sophia buys it anyway, tells herself it means nothing

2. **Party Arrival: The Look** (8 pages)
   - Jordan's house already packed: hockey team, girlfriends, wider school crew
   - Sophia arrives with Maya, immediately finds Ethan in crowd
   - He's Joker (full makeup), Becca is perfect Harley Quinn
   - He sees Sophia in hockey jersey, literally stops talking mid-sentence
   - Jordan notices, leans to whisper to teammate: "Oh man"

3. **Party Games: Truth Surfaces** (12 pages)
   - Group plays various party games, inhibitions lowering
   - Truth-or-dare circle, bottle spins, lands on Ethan
   - Question: "Who's the most important person in this room?"
   - Should be Becca, everyone expects Becca
   - Ethan looks at Becca, looks at Sophia, hesitates five seconds too long
   - Answers "Becca" but everyone heard the hesitation
   - Becca's smile doesn't reach eyes

4. **The Roof: Near Miss** (16 pages)
   - Ethan disappears, needs air
   - Maya to Sophia: "Go check on him"
   - Sophia finds him on roof, October cold, he's still in Joker makeup
   - "You should be inside." "So should you."
   - Conversation turns to performance: "Everyone expects me to be this person"
   - Sophia: "You don't have to perform for me. I knew you when you cried because you lost a tooth."
   - He laughs, and it's genuine
   - Moment shifts: "You're the only person who sees me"
   - Physical proximity increasing, he leans closer, she tilts up
   - Almost kiss—lips inches apart—he pulls back: "I can't. Becca doesn't deserve this."

   - Ethan drives Sophia home, silence heavy
   - Pull into driveway, engine running

   - "Maybe we should... I don't know, have some space? Not stop being friends, just—"

   - Neither moves for full minute
   - Finally she gets out, he watches her go inside
   - Both lie awake that night, hating the agreement they just made

6. **Three Days Later: The Break** (3 pages)
   - Montage: three days of distance
   - Passing in hallways, polite waves, no texting
   - Sophia miserable, throwing herself into badminton
   - Ethan playing badly at practice, coach yelling
   - Friday night: game without Sophia in stands
   - St. Paul's losing for first time this season
   - Second period: Ethan in locker room, grabs phone, texts Sophia

## Chapter Breakdown

### Chapter 1: "Costume" (20 pages)
- **Scene**: Pre-party costume selection (flashback)
- **Scene**: Party arrival, Ethan's reaction to hockey jersey
- **Development**: Attraction is visible, external
- **End**: Party games about to start

### Chapter 2: "Truth" (20 pages)
- **Scene**: Party games, escalating truth-or-dare
- **Development**: Ethan's hesitation reveals everything
- **Complication**: Becca noticed, others noticed
- **End**: Ethan goes to roof, Sophia follows

### Chapter 3: "Almost" (20 pages)
- **Scene**: Roof conversation, the near-kiss
- **Scene**: Silent drive home
- **Development**: They both know what they want and can't have
- **Cliffhanger Setup**: Agreement to have distance
- **End**: "Maybe space is good." (Reader knows this is a lie.)

### Chapter 4: "Distance" (20 pages)
- **Montage**: Three days of attempted distance
- **Development**: Both miserable, Ethan playing badly
- **Scene**: Friday game without Sophia, team losing
- **Cliffhanger**: His text: "Game tonight. You're not there. We're losing. Please come."
- **Final Line**: Sophia in pajamas, staring at text. Types: "Give me five minutes." Grabs jacket, runs out door. Freeze frame: her running.

## Cliffhanger Details
**Type**: The Pull (Gravity Wins)

They agreed to distance. It lasted three days. His text isn't subtle: "You're not there. We're losing." The implication: she's essential, not just nice to have around. Her response: dropping everything, running to game in pajamas and jacket. The cliffhanger is the inevitability—they can't stay away.

**Reader Question**: What happens when she gets there? Will he see her in the stands? Will they acknowledge this means something? What will Becca think?

**Why It Works**: Episode 3 cliffhangers should acknowledge the central tension while raising stakes. This one does both—they tried to resist, they failed immediately, and now everyone will see they failed.

## Emotional Goal
**Make readers feel**: The agony of "almost." The frustration of doing the "right thing" when it feels terrible. The inevitability of two people who belong together but can't find their way yet.

## What This Episode Makes Readers Feel
1. **Tension**: That roof scene should hurt (in best way)
2. **Frustration**: "Just break up with Becca!" (but understanding why he won't)
3. **Inevitability**: They can't stay away—it's impossible
4. **Anticipation**: What happens next? (Readers need Episode 4 immediately)
5. **Investment**: Even Becca becomes sympathetic—no villains here, just complicated hearts

# EPISODE 4: "Should Work"

## Basic Information
- **Episode Number**: S01E04
- **Page Count**: 80 pages (20,000 words)
- **Timeline**: November through early December
- **Primary Locations**: School hallways, badminton tournaments, group study sessions, The Forks, various "double date" locations, Ethan's truck (ongoing setting)
- **Weather**: November cold sets in, early winter, darkness by 5pm

## Synopsis
After the Halloween break in distance, Sophia and Ethan fall back into constant orbit—but now everyone's watching, and they're hyperaware. Enter Tyler Kim: smart, nice, genuinely interested in Sophia, perfect on paper. Maya and Jordan orchestrate group hangouts that are barely disguised double dates. Tyler is kind, conversation is good, he should work. But when Sophia's with Tyler, she's looking for Ethan. When Ethan sees Sophia with Tyler, something dark crosses his face that Becca definitely notices. Episode ends with Sophia agreeing to officially date Tyler (everyone's so happy for them!), and Ethan receiving text from Becca: "We need to talk." Readers know both these relationships are doomed, but the characters don't. Yet.

## Story Components

### Main Plot (60%): Tyler Kim Enters
Tyler Kim, St. Paul's academic star, student council president, generally great human, notices Sophia. Asks her to study, then to coffee, then to a movie. Maya encourages it ("He's perfect for you!"). Jordan and Maya engineer group hangouts: double dates that pair Ethan/Becca and Sophia/Tyler. On paper, it works. In reality, wrong pairs are falling for each other.

### Character Arc (25%): Sophia Performs "Right Choice"
Sophia tells herself Tyler is exactly what she should want: available, interested, age-appropriate lack of complication. She performs "girl on nice dates" but can't stop comparing everything Tyler does to what Ethan would do. Arc: Tries to want Tyler → realizes she's performing again → agrees to date him anyway (running from truth).

### Series Arc (15%): Jealousy Surfaces
Ethan can't hide his jealousy anymore. When Tyler puts arm around Sophia, Ethan's jaw clenches. When Tyler makes her laugh, Ethan finds excuse to leave room. Becca sees all of it. Jordan sees all of it. Even Tyler, sweet oblivious Tyler, starts to sense he's competing with someone.

## Romance Beats

### The Game Return (Chapter 1)
Sophia arrives at game (after his desperate text Episode 3 ending). He sees her in stands, visible relief. Team rally, they win. After game, through crowd, his eyes find hers: "You came." Everything unsaid in those two words.

### The Tyler Introduction (Chapter 2)
Tyler approaches Sophia after AP Physics, asks if she wants to study. She agrees (why not?). Study session is fine. He's smart, kind, gets her humor. She should feel something. She texts Maya after: "Study session with Tyler was good." Maya: "GOOD? Details!" Sophia realizes "good" is the problem.

### The Double Date Observation (Chapter 3)
Group outing: Ethan/Becca, Sophia/Tyler, Maya/Jordan, plus others. Supposed to be fun. Instead it's torture. Sophia watching Ethan with Becca. Ethan watching Sophia with Tyler. Both pretending they're not watching. Jordan mutters to Maya: "This is painful." Maya: "Right?"

### The Jealousy Slip (Chapter 3)
Tyler says something funny, Sophia laughs. Ethan, across table, literally stands up: "I need air." Becca follows him out. Through window, they can see them talking (arguing?). Ethan gestures, frustrated. Becca's face is sad, not angry. Tyler to Sophia: "Is he okay?" Sophia: "Yeah. He's fine." (Lie.)

### The Cliffhanger Setup (Chapter 4)
Tyler asks Sophia to Winter Formal (December dance). In front of friend group, including Ethan. Sophia should say yes—everyone expects yes, Tyler's great, why wouldn't she? She looks at Ethan. His face is carefully neutral. She says yes. Tyler's thrilled. Maya squeals. Jordan looks concerned. Ethan says "Congrats" and walks away. That night, text from Becca to Ethan: "We need to talk." He knows what's coming.

## Key Scenes

1. **Game Return: The Relief** (7 pages)
   - Sophia arrives at rink, pajamas under jacket, breathless
   - Finds seat in stands, team on ice between periods
   - Ethan skates out, scans crowd, finds her
   - Visible change in his posture: relief, focus, determination
   - Team rally, win game, celebrate on ice
   - In parking lot after, he finds her: "You came"
   - "You asked." Everything in the space between words.
