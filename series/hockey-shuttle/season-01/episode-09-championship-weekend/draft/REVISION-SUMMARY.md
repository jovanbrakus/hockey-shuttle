## DIALOGUE IMPROVEMENTS ACROSS ALL CHAPTERS

### General Principles Applied:
1. **More sentence fragments in emotional moments**
   - "I'm getting—the hearing's in ten minutes" instead of complete sentences
   - "Mom, what if they actually—what if it's real?"

2. **Character-specific speech patterns**
   - Sophia: Science metaphors, self-correction precision
   - Ethan: Hockey metaphors, quieter, more internal
   - Maya: Gen Z markers, "That's actually so messed up"
   - Jordan: Consistent "man" usage, supportive friend voice

3. **Code-switching between contexts**
   - Sophia with parents vs. with Ethan vs. in court
   - Formal register in testimony, looser at party

4. **More overlapping/interrupted dialogue**
   - Especially in family scenes and party
   - Realistic conversation flow

5. **Teen stress responses**
   - Trailing off when overwhelmed
   - Overuse of certain words when anxious
   - Less articulate, more physical expression

### Specific Examples:
**BEFORE:** "I have loved you since I was nine years old."
**AFTER:** "I've been—God, since we were nine, Soph. Since the beginning."

**BEFORE:** "I need to be free to choose my own future."
**AFTER:** "I just—I need to make my own calls here. My own plays."

**BEFORE:** "Choosing my own future means choosing all of it."
**AFTER:** "You can't segment variables like that. School, sport, Ethan—they're all part of the same equation."
