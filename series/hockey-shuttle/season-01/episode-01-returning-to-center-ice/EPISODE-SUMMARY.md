## Key Relationships Established

### Sophia & Ethan
- Deep childhood bond (age 9) - best friends who understood each other
- 8-year separation after his move to Winnipeg for AAA hockey
- Unexpected reunion at St. Paul's High School
- Immediate chemistry despite years apart
- He calls her "Soph" - only he uses that nickname
- Both intellectually brilliant (top students in calc)
- He values her for seeing the "real him" beneath the performance
- She's falling for him (realizes by end of episode)
- He's with Becca but clearly values Sophia's presence deeply

### Sophia & Becca
- Becca is Ethan's girlfriend (together since sophomore year, ~1.5 years)
- Kind but aware something's different about Sophia
- Student council president, genuinely nice person
- Not openly threatened but observant
- "Relationship goals" according to other students
- Sophia feels like an intruder around her

### Sophia & Maya (Badminton Team)
- Maya Foster: team captain, direct, welcoming
- Competitive match (Sophia wins 11-9) establishes mutual respect
- Potential real friend outside Ethan's world
- Also Sarah and Claire from the team
- They warn her hockey players are "separate universe"
- Offer genuine friendship and belonging

### Ethan & His World
- Tyler Hayes: best friend since age 9, teammate
- Mrs. Hayes: Tyler's mom, maternal figure to Ethan
- Hockey team: Marcus, Jamie, Connor, Alex
- Scouts watching him (NHL prospect)
- Parents who sacrificed everything for his hockey career
- Pressure to be "Ethan Price, hockey player" vs. just Ethan
- Secret dream: engineering at MIT/Caltech

1. **Identity vs. Performance**
   - Ethan struggling with who he is vs. who everyone needs him to be
   - "I don't know if there's any 'just me' left"

2. **Forces in Equilibrium** (Sophia's metaphor)
   - Her recurring thought when anxious
   - Physics as lens for understanding life
   - By end of episode: forces are NOT in equilibrium anymore

3. **Childhood Bonds**
   - Can you recapture what you had as kids?
   - How do people change in 8 years?
   - "No time passed at all" vs. "completely different"

4. **Sacrifice & Obligation**
   - Ethan's parents sacrificing for his hockey
   - His obligation to fulfill their investment
   - Dreams deferred for others' expectations

5. **Belonging**
   - Sophia seeking where she fits: badminton team, friend groups, Ethan's world
   - The tension between different social spheres

6. **Inevitable Heartbreak**
   - Sophia knowing this will hurt but unable to stop
   - Choosing connection over safety
