## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Episode 10 serves as a strong Season 1 finale with good emotional resonance and character development. However, there are several significant continuity issues that need addressing, missed opportunities for callbacks, and timeline inconsistencies that could confuse readers. The episode successfully resolves the NHL offer cliffhanger while setting up Season 2, but some character arcs and plot threads from earlier episodes are either dropped or inadequately addressed.

**Overall Continuity Grade:** B+ (Good, but needs refinement)

## CRITICAL CONTINUITY ERRORS

### 1. TIMELINE COLLAPSE (MAJOR ISSUE)

**ERROR:** Episode 10 compresses the timeline in ways that contradict the Episode 9 cliffhanger and season planning documents.

**Episode 9 Ending (May 15th, 11:31 PM):**
- NHL offer made: "Decision by June 1st"
- 17 days to decide
- Graduation scheduled for May 29th (14 days away)

**Episode 10 Timeline Issues:**

**Chapter 1 (Missing):**
- Episode jumps directly to late May/early June without addressing the immediate aftermath of the NHL call
- No scene showing Ethan's initial reaction or the next day's conversation
- The NHL decision deadline (June 1st) is never explicitly shown being made

**Chapter 3 (Prom - June 7th):**
- "Saturday after his MIT decision—June 2nd"
- This implies the MIT decision was made June 1st or 2nd
- But we never SEE this decision being made in real-time
- The cliffhanger resolution happens off-screen

**Chapter 4 (Graduation - June 28th):**
- Graduation happens June 28th
- Episode 9 said graduation was May 29th
- **30-DAY DISCREPANCY**

**RECOMMENDATIONS:**
1. Add Chapter 1 showing the immediate aftermath of the NHL call (May 16th morning)
2. Show Ethan making the NHL decision by June 1st deadline
3. Either correct graduation date to May 29th OR explain why it was moved to June 28th
4. Create a clear timeline bridge from Episode 9's cliffhanger to Episode 10's resolution

## CONCLUSION

Episode 10 "Commence" is a solid season finale with strong emotional beats and character development. However, it suffers from critical continuity errors (name inconsistency, timeline gaps) and structural issues (missing opening chapters, off-screen cliffhanger resolution) that need addressing before publication.

The episode successfully transitions from Winnipeg to MIT and sets up Season 2 conflicts, but readers invested in the Episode 9 cliffhanger may feel cheated by the off-screen resolution of Ethan's NHL decision.

**Recommended Action:** Moderate revision required
- Fix critical errors (name, timeline)
- Add missing chapters (1-2)
- Enhance Robert's arc
- Remove repetitive second NHL call
- Strengthen cliffhanger focus

With these revisions, Episode 10 could be an excellent season finale that honors the journey of Season 1 while launching compelling Season 2 arcs.
