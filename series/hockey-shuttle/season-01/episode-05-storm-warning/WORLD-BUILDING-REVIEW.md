## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**Overall Assessment**: STRONG with opportunities for enhancement

Episode 5 successfully expands the series world beyond Winnipeg, introducing authentic cross-border travel, blizzard atmosphere, and tournament settings. The road trip structure provides effective world-building through movement and contrast. However, sensory grounding varies across chapters, and some locations lack the vivid specificity established in earlier episodes.

### Strengths
- Authentic winter travel atmosphere and blizzard progression
- Effective use of setting as emotional amplifier (storm = internal chaos)
- Strong contrast between public/private spaces
- Highway 75 corridor accurately portrayed
- Hotel room intimacy well-developed

### Weaknesses
- Grand Forks lacks distinct personality beyond "generic tournament city"
- Sensory details inconsistent (strong in some scenes, absent in others)
- Border crossing feels rushed given its symbolic weight
- Bell MTS Iceplex scene could be more grounded
- Weather progression needs tighter technical accuracy

**Weather Accuracy Check** (IMPORTANT):

You describe:
- January 5-6, southern Manitoba/North Dakota
- Blizzard warning upgraded
- 18 inches overnight
- Highways closed

**REALISTIC?** Yes, but needs specifics:

January blizzards in this region typically feature:
- **Snow accumulation**: 12-24 inches possible
- **Wind**: 30-50 mph gusts (this creates the real danger)
- **Wind chill**: -30°F to -40°F (-34°C to -40°C)
- **Visibility**: Near zero (whiteout conditions)
- **Duration**: 12-24 hours
- **Highway closures**: Common, especially Highway 75 (exposed)

**Missing Sensory Elements** (IMPORTANT):
```
The storm should SOUND:
- Wind howling (not just mentioned, but described)
- Windows rattling in frames
- Building creaking
- Eerie silence when wind pauses
- Snow hitting window like sand

The storm should FEEL:
- Temperature drop even inside
- Draft from windows
- Static electricity from dry air
- Pressure change (ears popping)

The storm should LOOK:
- White-on-white (no depth perception)
- Snow horizontal, not falling down
- Drifts forming in real-time
- Complete disorientation when looking outside
- Everything buried—cars, signs, familiar landmarks gone
```

- Friday midnight: Full blizzard conditions
