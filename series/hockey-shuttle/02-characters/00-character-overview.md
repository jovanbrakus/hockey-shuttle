# Character Overview - Hockey Shuttle Season 1
## Complete Cast Guide

---

## Main Protagonists

### Sophia Chen - The Returning Friend
**Role**: Co-protagonist, POV character
**Age**: 17, senior year
**Core Conflict**: Finding authentic belonging in new city while navigating feelings for Ethan

**Key Traits**:
- Brilliant but self-doubting
- Athletic (badminton) + Academic (physics/math)
- Switched from hockey to badminton when Ethan left at age 9
- Analytical overthinker with fragmented internal monologue
- Comfortable on court, anxious in hallways

**Season 1 Arc**: Outsider → earns belonging → confronts real feelings → chooses authenticity

**Full Profile**: `/02-characters/main-cast/01-sophia-chen.md`

---

### Ethan Price - The Performer
**Role**: Co-protagonist, POV character
**Age**: 17, senior year
**Core Conflict**: Living everyone else's dreams vs. choosing his own path

**Key Traits**:
- Star AAA hockey player (center, captain)
- Secretly brilliant (wants engineering, not NHL)
- People-pleaser performing constant success
- Dual-track internal monologue (public vs. private self)
- Popular but profoundly lonely

**Season 1 Arc**: Perfect performance → cracks showing → breaking point → chooses truth

**Full Profile**: `/02-characters/main-cast/02-ethan-price.md`

## Key Supporting Characters

### Becca Martinez - The Ex-Girlfriend
**Role**: Ethan's girlfriend (14 months), student council president
**Age**: 17, senior year
**Important**: NOT THE VILLAIN - she's a real person in a dying relationship

**Key Traits**:
- Perfect image, controlled exterior
- Genuinely kind but image-obsessed
- Loves the idea of Ethan, not the real him
- Aware relationship is ending before he says it
- Gets own growth arc, not just obstacle

**Season 1 Arc**: Denial → recognition → mature choice → own future

**Full Profile**: `/02-characters/recurring/01-becca-martinez.md`

---

### Maya Foster - The Best Friend
**Role**: Badminton team captain, Sophia's earned friendship
**Age**: 17, senior year

**Key Traits**:
- Fierce competitor, #1 singles player
- Direct communication, cuts through BS
- Caribbean heritage, protective of her sport
- Represents authentic belonging path
- Calls Sophia out on avoiding feelings

**Season 1 Arc**: Skeptical captain → genuine friend → own vulnerability journey

**Full Profile**: `/02-characters/recurring/03-maya-foster.md`

---

### Jordan Nakamura - The Bridge
**Role**: Ethan's teammate, becomes Sophia's friend, observer/truth-teller
**Age**: 17, senior year

**Key Traits**:
- Hockey defenseman + digital artist
- Dry humor, wry observations
- Sees through everyone's performance
- Models integrated life (doesn't choose one identity)
- Protective of both protagonists

**Season 1 Arc**: Observer → bridge → truth-teller → own choice about art/hockey

**Full Profile**: `/02-characters/recurring/02-jordan-nakamura.md`

---

### Tyler Kim - The Wrong-But-Right Guy
**Role**: Brief romantic attempt (Episodes 4-6), Ethan's teammate
**Age**: 17, senior year

**Key Traits**:
- Genuinely nice, objectively perfect
- Zero chemistry with Sophia
- Represents "should work on paper"
- Mature about breakup
- Forces both protagonists to confront real feelings

**Season 1 Arc**: Setup → dating → mutual recognition → stays friends → finds Morgan

**Full Profile**: `/02-characters/recurring/05-tyler-kim.md`

## Parent Characters

### The Price Family (Ethan's Parents)
**Robert Price**: Former college hockey player, living through Ethan
**Jennifer Price**: Hockey mom whose identity is Ethan's success

**Dynamic**: United front of pressure, love conditional on achievement
**Impact**: Primary antagonistic force (not villains, but harm through "support")
**Season 1**: Increasing pressure → confrontation about MIT/Sophia → unresolved

---

### The Chen-Russell Family (Sophia's Parents)
**Dr. Lisa Chen**: Research scientist, MIT grad, achievement-oriented but healthy
**Mark Russell**: Tech director, work-life balance advocate, feels guilty about move

**Dynamic**: Supportive but too hands-off, miss Sophia's loneliness
**Impact**: Provide freedom Ethan wants, but Sophia needs more connection
**Season 1**: Well-meaning distance → recognize struggles → better communication

## Thematic Character Functions

### Identity & Authenticity
- **Sophia**: Multiple identities (athlete + brain) = strength not division
- **Ethan**: Performance vs. real self, choosing truth
- **Jordan**: Models integrated life, proof it's possible
- **Maya**: Independence vs. connection, strength through vulnerability

### Love & Relationships
- **Sophia/Ethan**: Right person, complicated timing, earned connection
- **Ethan/Becca**: Performance of relationship vs. authentic connection
- **Sophia/Tyler**: "Should work" vs. actual compatibility

### Pressure & Expectations
- **Ethan's Parents**: Pressure masked as support, conditional love
- **Sophia's Parents**: Trust that misses loneliness
- **Social expectations**: Popularity, perfection, predetermined paths

### Belonging
- **Sophia**: Earned vs. inherited belonging
- **Ethan**: Popular but lonely
- **Maya**: Creating authentic community
- **Everyone**: Finding people who see all of you

## Writing Guidelines

### Character Consistency
- Check voice guide before writing dialogue
- Review Want/Need/Lie/Truth for motivation
- Maintain distinct speech patterns
- Track emotional state through season

### Relationship Evolution
- Show changes gradually across episodes
- Impact of choices ripples to all relationships
- No relationship exists in isolation
- Parents and friends notice changes

### Avoid Stereotypes
- Becca is not a mean girl villain
- Parents are not evil or useless
- Athletes are not stupid
- Smart kids are not just academics
- Popularity doesn't equal shallow

### Authentic Teens
- Emotions feel huge and immediate
- Identity exploration and questioning
- Peer opinions carry enormous weight
- Desire for independence + need for support
- Humor as defense mechanism
- Code-switching between social contexts

## Using These Characters

### For Episode Writing
1. Check character profile for current emotional state
2. Review voice guide for authentic dialogue
3. Consult relationship dynamics for interactions
4. Track arc progression through season
5. Maintain consistency with established patterns

### For Continuity
- Reference character profiles between episodes
- Track emotional evolution
- Note relationship status changes
- Keep parent conflicts consistent
- Build toward season finale kiss

### For Authenticity
- Real teens, not stereotypes
- Complex motivations, not simple
- Flaws that matter, not cute quirks
- Growth earned, not sudden
- Relationships messy, not clean
