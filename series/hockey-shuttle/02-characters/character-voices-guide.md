# Character Voices & Dialogue Guide
## The Boy Who Knew Me First Series

This guide captures the distinct voice patterns, speech rhythms, and internal monologue styles for each main character. Use this to maintain authentic, differentiated voices throughout the series.

---

## SOPHIA CHEN - The Analytical Overthinker

### Internal Monologue Signature
- **Style**: Fragmented thoughts, mathematical/scientific metaphors, self-questioning
- **Rhythm**: Start-stop, spiraling, calculations interrupted by emotion
- **Key Traits**: Second-guesses interpretations, analyzes social dynamics like problems to solve

### Sample Internal Voice
*"Variables: new school, zero friends except the one person who knew me before I became this version of myself, badminton team that respects skill but not yet me personally, and Ethan Price looking at me like—no. Variables don't include how he looks at me. That's not a variable. That's a... that's a distraction from the actual equation. Which is: how do I stop being the new girl when everyone else has been writing their story together for three years and I'm just—"*

### Dialogue Patterns
**When Comfortable** (with Ethan, Maya, later Jordan):
- Full sentences
- Humor emerges
- Sports metaphors slip out
- Smart without apologizing for it
- Questions are genuine, not defensive

**When Guarded** (new people, uncomfortable situations):
- Trails off mid-sentence
- Over-explains then catches herself
- Deflects with information dumps
- Quieter, more formal vocabulary
- Ends statements with questions (seeking validation)

### Signature Phrases & Tells
- "Okay, so..." (buying time to think)
- "That's not—I mean—" (self-correcting)
- "Sorry, habit" (when sports/science takes over)
- Uses "actually" frequently when nervous
- Physics/badminton metaphors when stressed

### Sample Dialogue Exchanges

**Sophia (to Ethan, comfortable)**:
"You're reading the play wrong. No, listen—Jessica's setting up for the cross, not going straight. See how she's positioning? It's like when you—sorry, hockey brain. But same concept."

**Sophia (to new classmate, guarded)**:
"Yeah, I just moved here. From Millbrook, it's about four hours north. Small town, you probably haven't—anyway, yes, I'm new. Obviously new. Since it's October and everyone already has their... yeah."

**Sophia (internal, about Ethan)**:
*"He's still dating Becca. That's a fact. Facts don't change because I want them to. And I don't want them to. I don't. I want him to be happy, which he is, with his girlfriend, who is perfect and has been here the whole time and didn't disappear for seven years. These are just facts. Variables I can't change. So why does my chest feel like I'm holding my breath underwater every time I see them together?"*

## ETHAN PRICE - The Compartmentalized Performer

### Internal Monologue Signature
- **Style**: Dual-track thinking (what he should think vs. what he actually thinks)
- **Rhythm**: Public performance narration interrupted by buried truth
- **Key Traits**: Hyper-aware of being watched, toggles between personas

### Sample Internal Voice
*"Smile. That's what captains do, they smile and make everyone feel good about the game even when you lost the face-off in your own zone and nearly cost the team a goal. 'Good hustle, Carter.' See? Positive reinforcement. That's leadership. That's what Coach wants, what Dad wants, what everyone—and there's Sophia in the stands again. Top left corner, same seat. She came. Why does that make me feel more seen than anything else today? Stop. Focus. You have a girlfriend. Who you care about. Who's right there waiting for you, and you need to—but Soph's leaving already, and I didn't even get to—stop stop stop."*

### Dialogue Patterns
**Performance Mode** (teammates, most social situations, with parents):
- Smooth, encouraging
- Uses names frequently (connection strategy)
- Compliments flow easily
- Questions to deflect from himself
- "We" language for team solidarity

**Real Self** (rare, mostly with Sophia, sometimes Jordan):
- Sentences get longer, more complex
- Vulnerability leaks through
- Questions are actual questions
- Metaphors become specific
- Voice drops, pace slows

### Signature Phrases & Tells
- "It's fine" (it's never fine)
- "I'm good" (when he's drowning)
- "What do you think?" (deflection)
- Calls people by name constantly
- Runs hand through hair when lying

### Sample Dialogue Exchanges

**Ethan (to teammates, performance)**:
"That's what I'm talking about! You see that pass? Jordan, that was textbook. We run that play Friday, Riverside won't know what hit them. Tyler, same energy, keep it up."

**Ethan (to parents, performing)**:
"Yeah, the scout from Michigan was there. I think it went well. Coach said he was impressed with my vision on the ice. I'll keep working on conditioning like you said, Dad. MIT application? I'll get to it this weekend, between practice and the game. Don't worry."

**Ethan (to Sophia, real self emerges)**:
"Do you ever feel like you're watching your own life happen? Like you're doing everything right, hitting all the marks, and everyone's telling you how great you're doing, but you just feel... I don't know. Tired? No, not tired. Empty? That sounds dramatic. Forget it."

**Ethan (internal, during Becca conversation)**:
*"She's talking about spring formal. Dress colors, dinner reservations, the perfect photo spots. And I'm nodding, I'm engaged, I'm being a good boyfriend, but in my head I'm solving the physics problem Soph and I were working on earlier, seeing the momentum equations in the air above Becca's head, wondering if Soph figured out the error in the third calculation, wondering when I started thinking about physics more than about my actual girlfriend, wondering when I became this person who—'Ethan, are you listening?' Yes. Always. That's the problem."*

## MAYA FOSTER - The Direct Competitor

### Internal Monologue Signature
- **Style**: Match analysis applied to life, competitive assessment of everything
- **Rhythm**: Staccato observations, quick judgments, decisive
- **Key Traits**: Evaluates people like opponents, strategic thinking, protective

### Sample Internal Voice
*"New girl's got speed but her court positioning is weird. Hockey stance, has to be. Can't unlearn muscle memory that deep. Interesting. Most people come in trying to prove themselves, but she's just... working. Not performing. Not asking for approval. Might actually respect this one. If she earns it."*

### Dialogue Patterns
**General**:
- Short, declarative sentences
- Cuts through bullshit immediately
- Caribbean phrases when emotional
- Rarely softens statements
- Questions are challenges or genuine curiosity (no in-between)

**When Vulnerable** (rare, with Sophia later, with Marcus):
- Still direct but quieter
- Longer sentences
- Uses "you know?" seeking connection
- Admits uncertainty
- Voice softens but doesn't lose edge

### Signature Phrases & Tells
- "Real talk" (about to tell uncomfortable truth)
- "Here's the thing" (analysis incoming)
- "I'm just saying" (after saying something blunt)
- Sports metaphors constantly
- "Take the shot" (advice and philosophy)

### Sample Dialogue Exchanges

**Maya (meeting Sophia)**:
"You're fast. Footwork's weird but reflexes are good. You play other sports? Hockey? Makes sense. I'm captain. That was decent. Fix the backhand drop, you might make varsity."

**Maya (to Sophia, calling her out)**:
"Stop. You like him, he likes you, you're both doing this noble suffering thing. It's boring. Be brave or don't, but stop pretending you don't want what you want. That's just lying to yourself."

**Maya (vulnerable, late season)**:
"You know what's wild? I'm good at everything on the court. I know exactly what to do, how to position, when to smash, when to drop. But off the court? I have no idea what I'm doing with people. And everyone assumes because I'm confident there, I'm confident everywhere. But I'm just... faking it. Like everyone else."

**Maya (internal, watching Sophia)** *"She's got this thing where she doesn't realize people actually want to know her. Like she thinks she has to earn every conversation. And maybe I get that because I do the same thing, just louder. Different armor, same protection. Might have to tell her that. Later. After she stops being stupid about the hockey boy."*

## JORDAN NAKAMURA - The Wry Observer

### Internal Monologue Signature
- **Style**: Detached commentary, gentle snark, pattern recognition
- **Rhythm**: Observational beats, slightly amused, occasionally worried
- **Key Traits**: Narrates scenes like movie, protective humor, sees through performance

### Sample Internal Voice
*"And there it is. Ethan doing the thing where he pretends everything's fine while slowly drowning. Classic Price move. Should I say something? Probably. Will I? Probably not. He's got to figure this out himself, and honestly, watching someone finally choose themselves is kind of beautiful when you're not the one suffering through it. Dark? Maybe. Accurate? Definitely."*

### Dialogue Patterns
**General**:
- Deadpan delivery
- Dry humor as default
- References art and sports equally
- "Dude" and "man" as punctuation
- Understatement for emphasis

**When Serious** (rare but impactful):
- Drops the humor
- Direct eye contact (mentioned by others)
- Longer statements
- No deflection
- Usually one-on-one

### Signature Phrases & Tells
- "I'm just gonna stay out of this" (then gets involved)
- "Not my circus, not my monkeys" (his circus, his monkeys)
- "Dude, I love you, but..." (hard truth incoming)
- Art metaphors when explaining feelings
- Taps rhythmically when processing

### Sample Dialogue Exchanges

**Jordan (to teammates)**:
"Yeah, practice was brutal. No, I can't make the party, I've got this design thing. It's art homework, you can chirp me about it. I've heard them all."

**Jordan (to Ethan, calling him out)**:
"Dude, you're playing hurt in life. You can't keep making everyone comfortable while you're suffocating. That's not loyalty, that's just slow-motion disaster. And I say this as someone who loves you: figure it out."

**Jordan (to Sophia, bridging worlds)**:
"Here's the thing about Ethan. He's been trained to make everyone happy since he was nine. You're the first person in years who wants to know what he actually thinks. That's not nothing. But he's still with Becca, so... complicated."

**Jordan (internal, watching drama unfold)**:
*"If this were a painting, it'd be all tension lines—Ethan looking at Sophia, Becca looking at Ethan, Sophia looking anywhere but at Ethan, me wondering why I signed up for this emotional labor. Oh right, because they're my friends and I apparently hate watching good people make themselves miserable. Should've stayed in art room."*

## BECCA MARTINEZ - The Cracking Performance

### Internal Monologue Signature
- **Style**: List-making, control attempts, anxiety beneath polish
- **Rhythm**: Organized thoughts interrupted by fear/doubt
- **Key Traits**: Narrates life like directing a scene, controlling details when losing control

### Sample Internal Voice
*"Friday night dinner with Ethan: reservation at Rosso's, table by the window for photos, conversation topics prepped (college, hockey, spring formal). He'll smile, I'll laugh, everything will look perfect. And maybe if it looks perfect enough, it'll feel perfect again. Maybe if I schedule us correctly, we'll remember why we work. Maybe if I just... god, when did I become the girl who has to plan spontaneity?"*

### Dialogue Patterns
**Performance Mode** (most of the time):
- Upbeat with subtle edge
- "We" instead of "I"
- Seeks agreement ("right?")
- Plans and coordinates constantly
- Sophisticated vocabulary in groups

**Real Self** (rare, fleeting moments):
- Trails off uncertainly
- Questions everything
- Voice drops
- Admits doubt
- Actually asks instead of directs

### Signature Phrases & Tells
- "Right?" (seeking validation)
- "That's perfect" (when things aren't)
- "We should..." (attempting control)
- "It's fine" (echoing Ethan's lie)
- Adjusts hair when anxious

### Sample Dialogue Exchanges

**Becca (to friends, performing)**:
"So I'm thinking coordinating colors for the photos—nothing matchy but elevated casual. Morgan, that burgundy top, Tyler that jacket. Perfect. We'll look amazing."

**Becca (to Ethan, sensing distance)**:
"You've been quiet. Is it hockey stress? Scouts? You can talk to me, that's what I'm here for. We're a team, right?"

**Becca (rare vulnerable moment)**:
"Do you ever feel like you're playing a part? Like you know what you're supposed to want and do and say, but sometimes you wonder if you actually... sorry, that's dumb. Forget it."

**Becca (internal, watching Ethan with Sophia)**:
*"Just studying. That's all. Ethan helps everyone with homework. But he doesn't laugh like that with Tyler. Doesn't do that hair thing because he's excited, not stressed. Doesn't look at anyone else like they're the most interesting person alive. Should I go over there? Remind him I exist? Except what would I even say that doesn't sound desperate? This is my perfect senior year dissolving and I don't know how to stop it."*

## Voice Differentiation Quick Reference

### Sentence Structure
- **Sophia**: Fragments, self-interruption, spiraling
- **Ethan**: Smooth (performance) vs. complex (real)
- **Maya**: Short declaratives, staccato
- **Jordan**: Measured, observational beats
- **Becca**: Organized lists interrupted by anxiety

### Metaphor Style
- **Sophia**: Physics, badminton, equations
- **Ethan**: Hockey plays, team dynamics
- **Maya**: Sports competition, match strategy
- **Jordan**: Art, design, visual composition
- **Becca**: Directing, staging, photography

### Humor Type
- **Sophia**: Self-deprecating, accidentally funny
- **Ethan**: Deflecting, people-pleasing
- **Maya**: Blunt observations, direct
- **Jordan**: Dry, deadpan, wry
- **Becca**: Performative (rarely genuine)

### Vulnerability Markers
- **Sophia**: Trails off, apologizes, over-explains
- **Ethan**: Longer sentences, voice drops, admits confusion
- **Maya**: Quieter, uses "you know?", softens edge
- **Jordan**: Drops humor, direct statements
- **Becca**: Voice breaks, admits doubt, questions self

### Physical Tells (Mentioned in Dialogue Tags)
- **Sophia**: Pulls hair into ponytail, taps racquet strings
- **Ethan**: Runs hand through hair, forced vs. real smile
- **Maya**: Crosses arms, intense eye contact
- **Jordan**: Rhythmic tapping, observes before speaking
- **Becca**: Adjusts hair, perfect posture cracks

## Dialogue Writing Checklist

### Before Writing Any Dialogue, Ask:
1. **Who's speaking?** (Check their patterns above)
2. **Who are they talking to?** (Performance vs. real mode)
3. **What are they hiding?** (Subtext beneath words)
4. **What do they want in this moment?** (Immediate goal)
5. **What can't they say?** (The real truth)

### Character Voice Test:
Cover the character name. Can you tell who's speaking?
- If not, adjust vocabulary, rhythm, or metaphor style
- Each character should be identifiable by voice alone

### Authenticity Check:
- Would a real 17-year-old say this?
- Is it too adult/too young?
- Does it sound written or spoken?
- Would they say this to this person?
- What's the emotional truth beneath words?

## Sample Multi-Character Scene

**Setting: Library study session, Episode 5**
**Characters: Ethan, Sophia, Jordan**

---

**Ethan**: "Okay, so if you solve for acceleration first, the rest of the problem is just—"

**Sophia**: "Wait wait wait. You're doing it the long way again. Look—" *reaches for pencil* "—if you use the kinematic equation directly, you skip like three steps."

**Ethan**: *laughing* "Of course you see the shortcut. You always did."

**Jordan**: *not looking up from sketchbook* "You two are aggressively nerdy and it's both adorable and painful to watch."

**Sophia**: "We're solving physics problems. That's literally what we're supposed to be doing."

**Jordan**: "Uh-huh. Sure. That's definitely all that's happening here."

**Ethan**: "What's that supposed to mean?"

**Jordan**: "Nothing, man. Just observing. Purely academic observation." *finally looks up* "Hey, real question though—does Becca know you're here?"

**Ethan**: *posture shifts slightly* "I texted her. She's at student council."

**Jordan**: "Cool, cool. Just checking. Since last time she asked where you were and you said 'library' but left out the 'with Sophia' part."

**Sophia**: *quiet* "I should go. I've got badminton conditioning."

**Ethan**: "You don't have to—"

**Jordan**: "Yes, she does, practice is mandatory. And you have that thing with Coach. We all have places to be and absolutely no one is avoiding anything."

**Sophia**: *standing, gathering books* "Same time Thursday?"

**Ethan**: *too quickly* "Yes. Yeah, Thursday's good."

**Jordan**: *deadpan* "This is fine. Everything is fine. No emotional subtext happening whatsoever."

**Sophia**: "You're weird, Nakamura."

**Jordan**: "I'm the weird one? That's rich. You two are over here doing physics foreplay—"

**Ethan**: "Okay, that's enough—"

**Sophia**: *laughing despite herself* "Physics foreplay, oh my god—"

**Jordan**: "—and I'm the weird one for pointing it out. Sure. I'll accept that."

**Ethan**: *to Sophia* "Ignore him. Thursday?"

**Sophia**: "Thursday." *leaves*

**Jordan**: *once she's gone* "Dude."

**Ethan**: "Don't."

**Jordan**: "I didn't say anything."

**Ethan**: "You're thinking it loud."

**Jordan**: "You know what I'm thinking louder? That you're supposed to meet Becca in ten minutes and you just scheduled another study session with Sophia without checking. That's what I'm thinking loud."

**Ethan**: *quiet* "It's just studying."

**Jordan**: "Yeah. And my digital paintings are just homework. We both know that's bullshit."

---

**Analysis of Above Scene**:
- Each character maintains distinct voice
- Subtext clear beneath dialogue
- Physical actions match characters
- Conflicts emerge naturally
- Humor and tension balanced
- Authentic teen speech patterns
- Reveals character through conversation
