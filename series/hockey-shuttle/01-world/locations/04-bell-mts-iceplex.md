# Bell MTS Iceplex - Elite Hockey Facilities

## Overview
**Location**: 365 Hargrave Street (downtown Winnipeg)
**Type**: State-of-the-art training facility, home to Winnipeg Jets practice
**Ice Surfaces**: 3 NHL-sized rinks
**Opened**: 2010 (renovated 2016)
**Character**: Where elite hockey happens, professional-level facilities for aspiring pros

## Why This Facility

### The AAA Reality
- Home to multiple AAA hockey programs
- NHL-level training available
- Jets players practice here (proximity to professional)
- Scouts regularly present
- Every detail designed for performance evaluation
- Not community rink atmosphere—this is business

### The Investment
- Ice time expensive (AAA team pays, parents supplement)
- Training packages $10,000+ per year
- Skills coaches additional
- Equipment, travel, tournaments
- Full-time commitment
- His family's primary investment in his future

## Sensory Details

### Sight
- **Ice**: Perfect white surface, blue lines crisp, reflections
- **Glass**: Puck marks, scratches, watching faces through
- **Lights**: Bright for evaluation, shadows eliminated
- **Boards**: Advertising, scuff marks, contact evidence
- **Players**: Color-coded practice jerseys, coordinated movement
- **Scoreboard**: Time counting down always
- **Stands**: Scattered observers, concentrated attention

### Sound
- **Skates**: Carving ice, stopping sprays, rhythm of stride
- **Pucks**: Hitting boards (hollow thunk), tape-to-tape (satisfying click), goals (post ring)
- **Whistles**: Coach controlling flow, sharp and commanding
- **Voices**: Calling plays, shouting corrections, encouragement and criticism
- **Zamboni**: Periodic resurfacing, grinding and flooding
- **Arena**: Echo making everything louder, intimate and exposed

### Smell
- **Ice**: Clean, cold, sharp in nose and lungs
- **Equipment**: Sweat-soaked, chemical fabric, broken-in leather
- **Locker Room**: Sweat, deodorant, tape adhesive, mildew
- **Cafe**: Coffee, overpriced pizza, popcorn
- **Lobby**: Mix of cold from rinks and warmth from heating
- **Nostalgia**: Same smells as Millbrook rink, universe apart

### Touch
- **Cold**: Seeping from ice surface, fighting with building heat
- **Boards**: Smooth impact surface, contact absorbed
- **Ice**: Blade edges gripping, puck on stick, snow spraying
- **Equipment**: Weight of gear, restriction and protection, second skin
- **Bench**: Cold metal, sitting between shifts, legs recovering
- **Glass**: Cold when leaning close, vibrating with impact

### Temperature
- **Rink Level**: 50-55°F (10-13°C), layers necessary
- **Viewing Areas**: Heated but drafty, cold seeps
- **Locker Room**: Warm, then cold from wet, then warm again
- **Ice Surface**: Cold radiating up, sweat cooling instantly
- **Lobby**: Overheated to compensate, removing layers

### Atmosphere
- **Professional**: Not fun, not recreational—this is work
- **Evaluative**: Always being watched, measured, judged
- **Competitive**: Against teammates, opponents, standards, self
- **Expensive**: Visible in every detail, investment demanding return
- **Exclusive**: Elite space, not everyone belongs
- **Pressurized**: Dreams and money and time compressed to moments
- **Masculine**: Traditional hockey culture, toughness valued
- **Isolating**: Even surrounded, performing alone
