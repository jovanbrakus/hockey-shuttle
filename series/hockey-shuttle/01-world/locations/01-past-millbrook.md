# PAST LOCATIONS - Millbrook, Ontario

## 1. Millbrook Community Arena
**"The Barn"**

### Physical Description
A single-pad arena built in 1982, with faded blue metal siding and a roof that leaks when it rains. The wooden boards are covered in decades of scratches and sponsor banners for local businesses that no longer exist. Penalty boxes smell like sweat-soaked equipment and orange slices.

### Sensory Details
- **Sight**: Fluorescent lights casting harsh shadows on ice, parents bundled in winter coats in barely-heated stands, breath visible even inside
- **Sound**: Skate blades carving ice, pucks hitting boards with hollow thunks, echoing voices in empty space, ancient Zamboni groaning
- **Smell**: Ice (sharp, clean), wet wool, Tim Hortons coffee from the lobby, hockey bag funk
- **Touch**: Biting cold from the ice surface, smooth dasher boards worn by thousands of small hands, rough wooden bench seats
- **Atmosphere**: Communal, everyone-knows-everyone energy. Parents volunteer for everything. Kids share equipment.

### Story Function
- **Flashbacks**: Where they first skated together, fell together, helped each other up
- **Emotional Core**: Represents innocence before ambition separated them
- **Contrast**: Shows how far he's come (AAA facilities vs The Barn)
- **Key Memory**: The day his parents announced the move while he sat in full gear, not understanding yet what he'd lose

### Specific Scenes
- **Age 5-6**: Parent-and-tot hockey, both wobbling on tiny skates
- **Age 7-8**: First real practices, her keeping up with the boys
- **Age 9**: His last practice before moving, both pretending it's fine

## 2. Millbrook Public School
**Grades K-8, brick building circa 1960**

### Physical Description
Two-story red brick with tall windows, radiator heat that clangs. Hallways smell like industrial cleaner and cafeteria pizza. Library is one small room with worn carpet and outdated encyclopedias. Science "lab" is just regular classroom with a sink.

### Sensory Details
- **Sight**: Student art covering walls, hand-painted murals in stairwells, trophy case with dusty awards from the 80s
- **Sound**: Sneakers squeaking on linoleum, announcements crackling over ancient PA system, voices echoing off high ceilings
- **Smell**: Cafeteria food, old books, cleaning supplies, pencil shavings
- **Touch**: Cold lockers, smooth hallway floors, sticky desks with decades of residue
- **Atmosphere**: Wholesome, slightly dated, everyone-knows-everyone

### Specific Spaces

## 3. Maple Street Neighborhood
**Their Childhood Streets**

### Physical Description
Working-class neighborhood with small bungalows and split-levels from the 60s-70s. Detached garages, chain-link fences, kids' toys in front yards. Sidewalks buckled by tree roots. Basketball hoops on garage doors. Dogs barking at each other through fences.

### Her House (Then)
**114 Maple Street - Two-story split-level, blue siding**
- Driveway where dad taught them both to shoot pucks
- Basement rec room with air hockey table
- Backyard rink in winter (just flooded grass, not fancy)
- Her bedroom window facing the street, where she watched for him

### His House (Then)
**127 Maple Street - Bungalow, white with green trim**
- Same style as everyone else's house
- Hockey net in driveway
- Garage where his dad kept extra equipment
- Kitchen where his mom gave them snacks after skating

### Sensory Details
- **Sight**: Kids on bikes, hockey nets in driveways, seasonal decorations, cars in various states of repair
- **Sound**: Screen doors slamming, lawn mowers in summer, shovels scraping snow in winter, kids calling to each other
- **Smell**: Cut grass in summer, BBQ smoke, autumn leaves, snow and chimney smoke in winter
- **Touch**: Rough pavement perfect for road hockey, cold metal bike handles, smooth puck on asphalt
- **Atmosphere**: Safe, communal, kids running between houses

### Story Function
- **Before Class Divide**: Both families solidly middle-class
- **Physical Proximity**: Houses 13 addresses apart, always nearby
- **Casual Intimacy**: In and out of each other's houses constantly
- **Loss Marker**: What he left behind for River Heights mansion

### Key Memories
- **Winter Rinks**: Skating on backyard ice, no pressure, just fun
- **Street Hockey**: Summer evenings until parents called them in
- **Bike Rides**: To the arena, to school, to Tim Hortons for Timbits
- **The Moving Truck**: Loading his life away while she watched from her window

- **Innocence**: Before ambition, pressure, social hierarchy
- **Equality**: Both smart kids, both hockey kids, both just kids
- **Simplicity**: One school, one rink, one way of being
- **Loss**: What he traded for opportunity
- **Foundation**: Who they were before becoming who they are

### Color Palette

- Natural greens and golds (trees, grass, autumn)
- Winter whites and grays
- Tim Hortons red
- Arena ice blue

### Symbolic Weather
- **Autumn**: When he left (harvest, endings, change)
- **Winter Remembered**: Perfect in memory (Christmas lights, snow days)
- **Spring Lost**: He wasn't there when seasons changed
- **Frozen in Time**: In memory, always golden

## How Past Informs Present

### What She Remembers
- How easily they existed together
- No social hierarchies between them
- His laugh before pressure changed it
- Possibility before life got complicated

### What He Remembers
- Life before constant evaluation
- Friendship without social cost
- Her keeping up with him at everything
- Leaving her without saying what it meant

### The Gap Between
- Millbrook simplicity vs Winnipeg complexity
- Childhood intimacy vs teenage awkwardness
- Who they were vs who they've become
- What they lost vs what they gained
