# Family Homes - Where They Come From

## His Home: River Heights

### Address & Character
**Location**: Grosvenor Avenue, River Heights
**Type**: Renovated 1920s character home + modern addition
**Character**: Established wealth, tasteful display, hockey shrine
**Style**: Heritage exterior preserved, modern luxury interior

### The Exterior

**Architecture**:
- Two-and-a-half story Tudor revival
- Original dark brick with white stucco, exposed beams
- Slate roof (original, maintained)
- Mature elm trees (survivors of Dutch elm disease)
- Professional landscaping (seasonal flowers, perfect lawn)
- Circular driveway (recent addition, stone pavers)
- Three-car garage (detached, matched to house)
- Heated driveway and walkway (never shovel)

**The Neighborhood**:
- Wide streets with boulevards
- Sidewalks lined with century-old elms
- Mix of heritage homes and tasteful new builds
- Quiet (wealth muffles noise)
- Walkable to Corydon Avenue restaurants
- Christmas lights coordinated, tasteful
- Everyone knows property values

**First Impression** (Her Visit, Episode 4):
- Intimidating from street
- "This is where you live?"
- Millbrook house fits in garage
- Understanding his world differently
- Class gap made visible

### The Interior

**Entry Hall**:
- Original hardwood refinished
- Chandelier (crystal, understated)
- Staircase with dark wood banister
- Sight line through to great room
- Coat closet (organized, no clutter)
- Smell: expensive candles, clean, no cooking odors

**Main Floor**:

**Living Room** (front, formal):
- Heritage features preserved (fireplace, crown molding)
- Furniture: tasteful, uncomfortable, barely used
- Art on walls (local artists, tasteful)
- Coffee table books (design, travel, hockey history)
- Space for entertaining, not living

**Great Room** (back, addition):
- Open concept to kitchen
- Vaulted ceiling, pot lights
- Gas fireplace (modern, clean)
- Large sectional (this is where family actually sits)
- TV (large, for hockey games)
- Windows overlooking backyard
- Where family exists

**Kitchen**:
- Renovated 5 years ago (high-end appliances)
- Marble countertops, waterfall island
- His mom's domain (she actually cooks, despite wealth)
- Breakfast bar (where he eats usually)
- Pantry stocked perfectly
- Coffee station (espresso machine)
- Meal planning on display (his athletic diet)

**Dining Room**:
- Between living room and kitchen
- Table seats 10 (rarely full)
- China cabinet with dishes never used
- Where serious conversations happen
- The "talk about future" location
- Feeling: formal, evaluated

### Second Floor

**His Bedroom**:
- Larger than her family's living room
- King bed (unnecessary, but expected)
- Desk (homework, rarely used)
- Bookshelves (childhood books, dust, some new)
- Closet (walk-in, organized)
- Ensuite bathroom (luxury normal to him)
- Window seat overlooking front street

**The Revealing Details**:
- Hockey trophies (shelf, chronological)
- Team photos (walls, framed)
- Girlfriend's photo (nightstand, expected placement)
- Millbrook community rink photo (hidden in drawer)
- Elementary math competition trophy (closet shelf)
- Books he's actually reading (hidden under bed)
- Journal (locked, under mattress)
- Map of Ivy League schools (inside closet door)
- Her badminton tournament: circled on calendar (will he admit it?)

**The Divide**:
- Public half: hockey player, perfect boyfriend, future star
- Private half: questioning, remembering, hiding dreams
- What parents see vs what exists
- Performed identity vs actual self

**His Bathroom**:
- Separate shower and tub
- Double sink (why? he's one person)
- Products for athletic recovery
- Expensive cologne (gift from girlfriend)
- Old Irish Spring soap (actually prefers, hidden)
- Mirror: where he practices being who they expect

**Parents' Suite**:
- Master bedroom with sitting area
- His mom's office/dressing room
- Dad's closet (suits, business casual)
- Ensuite luxury
- Their domain, rarely entered by him

**Guest Room**:
- Always ready
- Rarely used
- Displays wealth through emptiness

### Third Floor

**Bonus Room/Loft**:
- His retreat (when bedroom not enough)
- Workout equipment
- Stick handling area
- Video game setup (rarely used anymore)
- Where he goes to think
- Farthest from parents

### Basement

**Finished Rec Room**:
- Bar area (parents entertain)
- Comfortable seating
- Big screen (playoff parties)
- Pool table
- Hockey memorabilia (NHL jerseys, signed sticks)
- His AAA jersey framed
- Trophy case (his achievements)
- Shrine to hockey investment

**His Training Space**:
- Gym equipment
- Weights, bench, cardio machine
- Mirrors for form
- Where he maintains body
- Early morning workouts
- Private maintenance of public asset

**Utility/Storage**:
- Organized chaos
- Christmas decorations labeled
- Hockey equipment storage (old gear, sentimental)
- Millbrook boxes (haven't opened in years)

### Backyard

**Summer**:
- Deck (composite, maintenance-free)
- BBQ area (high-end)
- Mature trees providing privacy
- Garden beds (professionally maintained)
- Lawn (perfect, treated, green)
- Space but not ostentatious

**Winter**:
- Deck cleared, heated
- Outdoor heater for gatherings
- No backyard rink (not anymore—outgrew it)
- Pristine snow (plowed immediately)
- Landscape lighting (tasteful)

### The Garage

**Three Bays**:
1. **Dad's Car**: Audi Q7 (practical luxury)
2. **Mom's Car**: Lexus RX (tasteful, safe)
3. **His Truck**: Silverado (graduation gift, "his" but they paid)

**Workshop Area**:
- Dad's tools (organized, rarely used)
- Skate sharpening station (this gets used)
- Bike storage (his old bikes kept)
- Millbrook moving boxes (still taped, never unpacked)

**The Hidden Past**:
- Boxes from Millbrook marked "donate"
- Never donated (mom's sentiment)
- His old life in cardboard
- Her house number written on box (114 Maple)
- Forgotten but not gone

### Family Dynamics in Space

**His Mom**:
- Home office in master suite
- Part-time consulting (flexible for his schedule)
- Kitchen as command center
- Managing his nutrition, schedule, future
- Loves through involvement
- Every tournament, every practice
- Investment manager of his potential

**His Dad**:
- Works downtown (finance/business)
- Home by 6 PM (prioritizes family)
- Great room recliner (his spot)
- Watches every game
- Quiet pride, high expectations
- Success defined clearly
- "We moved here for your opportunities"

**The Investment Visible**:
- House itself (chosen for school district, rink proximity)
- Neighborhood (networking, connections)
- Space dedicated to his training
- Schedule accommodating his needs
- Everything optimized for his success
- Love and pressure indistinguishable

### Key Scenes

**Episode 4: Her First Visit**:
- School project excuse (working together)
- Her: overwhelmed by space and wealth
- Him: seeing home through her eyes (embarrassed)
- Kitchen: his mom too interested, too welcoming
- His room: awkward, revealing
- Finding Millbrook photo (moment of connection)
- Understanding class divide clearly
- "It's just a house" (but it's not)

**Episode 6: The Family Dinner**:
- Parents grilling about grades, scouts, future
- Him: performing expected responses
- Girlfriend mentioned approvingly
- University hockey programs discussed
- Academic interests dismissed gently
- "Hockey first, then academics"
- His silence screaming
- After: him texting her (needing real conversation)

**Episode 9: The Confrontation**:
- Parents finding out time with her
- "Who is she? Why haven't we met her?"
- Girlfriend's parents called (social network)
- "You're distracted from priorities"
- "We sacrificed for your opportunities"
- His breaking point approaching
- "What if I want something different?"
- Silence (their confusion, disappointment)
- Him retreating to room, to truck, to her

**Type**: Two-story suburban standard, circa 1995
**Character**: Middle-class comfort, practical, lived-in, warm
**Style**: Vinyl siding, attached garage, identical to neighbors (with minor variations)

**Architecture**:
- Two-story builder standard
- Beige vinyl siding
- Attached double garage

- Driveway (asphalt, cracks filled)

- Basketball hoop on garage (rusty, used)
- Garden bed maintained by her mom (flowers, vegetables)

**The Neighborhood**:
- Post-1980s development
- Curved streets (modern planning)
- Similar houses, different colors

## Thematic Function

### Class Visibility
- Wealth evident in space, maintenance, options
- Not just income: cultural capital, connections, ease
- Her family: comfortable but choices considered
- His family: comfortable without thinking
- Different stress sources

### Family Values Through Space

**His Family**:
- Investment in future
- Individual success priority
- Space optimized for his development
- Love through opportunity provision
- Pressure disguised as support

**Her Family**:
- Balance in present
- Collective happiness
- Space shared equitably
- Love through presence
- Support without strings

### Home as Identity

**For Him**:
- House impresses, doesn't comfort
- Space for hockey player, not whole person
- Public vs private self division
- Wants out but doesn't know where to

**For Her**:
- House embraces, reflects fully
- Space for all interests
- Integrated self
- Comfort leaving because secure in return

### The Pull

**Him to Her Space**:
- Feeling seen completely
- Family accepting, not evaluating
- Can be uncertain, questioning, real
- Millbrook feeling recovered
- Who he was before pressure

**Her to His Space** (initially):
- Intimidated, class-conscious
- Understanding his constraints
- Sympathy for his pressure
- Seeing performance necessity
- Eventually: preference for her home together

**Where He Belongs (Supposedly)**:
- River Heights house
- Elite rink
- Top of social hierarchy
- Hockey future

**Where He Belongs (Actually)**:
- Her kitchen table
- Her family's acceptance
- Conversations about everything
- Future he chooses

**Where She Belongs (Supposedly)**:
- Invisible at school
- Supporting role
- Lower social tier
- Grateful for proximity

**Where She Belongs (Actually)**:
- Her authentic spaces
- Badminton court
- Library, learning
- Future she's building

**Where They Belong (Eventually)**:
- Spaces they choose together
- Free from expectations
- Accepted fully
- Building new home

By Episode 10, the contrast between homes reveals not just class difference but values difference, and he realizes her "smaller" house holds bigger life, her "normal" family offers extraordinary acceptance, and wealth without warmth is poverty in disguise.

Home isn't where you're from—it's where you're understood.
